11. How do the functions of money overcome the problems associated with barter?
Money acts as a medium of exchange (eliminating double coincidence of wants), a unit of account (standardizing value), and a store of value (allowing wealth to be saved).

12. What is seigniorage?
Seigniorage is the profit a government earns from issuing currency. It is the difference between the face value of the money and the cost to produce/distribute it.

11. Please define bank runs, contagion, and financial panics.
Bank Run: Simultaneous mass withdrawals due to fear of insolvency. Contagion: The spread of financial distress from one bank to others. Financial Panic: Widespread collapse of confidence in the financial system.

12. Identify examples of moral hazard and adverse selection for a person buying car insurance.
Adverse Selection: A high-risk driver is more likely to buy insurance than a safe one (occurs before purchase). Moral Hazard: Once insured, a driver may drive carelessly because they are protected (occurs after purchase).

11. Identify the differences between M1, M2, M3
M1 (Currency + Checking) is the most liquid. M2 includes M1 + Savings + CDs (less liquid). M3 includes M2 + large institutional deposits (least liquid).

12. Judge whether credit cards should be a form of money.
Credit cards are not money. They are a method of deferring payment. The card is an access tool to a line of credit, not a final store of value.
