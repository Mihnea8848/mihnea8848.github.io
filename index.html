SUBJECT 1: Money & Banking Exam

1. Rumors of a bank failing, even if not true, can become a self-fulfilling prophecy because:
(B) The rumors will cause people to not want to deposit in this bank.

2. What matters most during a bank run is:
(C) The liquidity of the bank.

3. Contagion is:
(A) The failure of one bank spreading to other banks through depositors withdrawing of funds.

4. An economic rationale for government protection of small investors is that:
(B) Many small investors cannot adequately judge the soundness of their bank.

5. The financial system is inherently more unstable than most other industries due to the fact that:
(C) A single firm failing in banking can bring down the entire system.

6. The government's role of lender of last resort:
(D) Was extended to non bank intermediaries through the Fed's emergency lending authority in the financial crisis in 2007, 2009.

7. A moral hazard situation arises in the lender of last resort function because:
(B) A central bank finds it difficult to distinguish illiquid from insolvent banks.

8. The existence of a lender of last resort creates moral hazard for bank managers because:
(B) They have an incentive to take too much risk in their operations.

9. One reason customers do not care about the quality of their bank's assets is:
(D) With deposit insurance, there isn't any real reason to care; their deposits are protected even if the bank fails.

10. Under the purchase-and-assumption method of dealing with a failed bank, the FDIC:
(B) Finds another bank to take over the insolvent bank.

11. The government's too-big-to-fail policy:
(C) Means that the intermediary is too big or too complex to shut down or sell in an orderly fashion without large and painful spillovers.

12. Banks can create money only:
(C) By making loans that result in additional deposits.

13. If people anticipate that the Fed is going to increase the money supply, they will:
(D) Sell bonds.

14. Which of the following is not a way that the Fed can affect the money supply?
(B) Taxes.

15. The liability side of a commercial bank's balance sheet includes all of the following except:
(C) Reserves.

16. Financial intermediaries include all of the following except:
(E) All of the above are financial intermediaries.

17. Because money serves as a medium of exchange, it eliminates:
(B) The need for a double coincidence of wants.

18. Barter is an inefficient means of exchange because:
(A) Barter transactions require a double coincidence of wants.

19. Suppose an increase in the supply of money occurs, while the demand for money is constant. According to the quantity theory of money, the price level will:
(B) Rise because as people try to spend excess money balances, prices are driven up.

20. Which of the following is correct? Central banks:
(D) Have the control of the money supply as a primary function.

21. Commercial banks are able to create money by:
(B) Making loans.

22. If banks have reserve requirements of 20 percent of their demand deposits, $100 million in excess reserves allows the banking system to create at most:
(C) $500 million in new money.

23. An individual bank in a banking system can lend no more than its excess reserves because:
(C) The bank risks the loss of reserves when borrowers spend the money.

24. Banks earn interest on all of the following except:
(D) Reserves deposited at the Fed.
